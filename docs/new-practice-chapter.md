# New Practice Chapter — beyond Newfoundland-as-subject

A companion to [practice-forward-brief.md](practice-forward-brief.md). Where that document
continues the Newfoundland body of work, this one asks: **what is the next chapter of the
practice when the rock is no longer the subject?**

Grounded in the same close reading (thesis, field guide, statements). The cleanest bridge
out is a sentence that already generalizes the thesis:

> "where you're at challenges where you're from."

The point of this chapter is to take the *questions and systems* — not the geography — to a
place Adam is *from*-less in, and test whether the method still produces wonder when there
is no ancestral ground underneath it.

- **Tone north star:** wonder, sublimity, tenderness (a direction may intentionally break
  tone — those are flagged).
- **Off-limits:** NFT drops, climate-campaign framing, tourism bait.
- **Assumptions:** highly technically proficient (can learn any new hardware/software);
  collaborators possible (Angela, family, strangers); all venues open (web, gallery, museum,
  book, public art, performance).

---

## What travels, what stays

**Inherit (portable):**
- **Telepresence method** — remote live data animating a here-and-now object or space; a
  general poetics of presence-at-a-distance for *any* inaccessible site.
- **The abundance-sublime** — a fixed frame + accumulated time producing awe from the
  quotidian (the Monet / Cloudberry engine).
- **Extracting a ritual from its architecture** to make intangible culture visible.
- **Collaboration with named people who hold authority over their own voice** (the
  oral-history ethic).
- **Home as ephemeral concept**, and the insider/outsider position as generative.
- **The surrogate device** — a camera/system standing in for an absent body.

**Leave behind (place-locked):** resettlement-as-literal-subject, the floating/ghost house,
"no great future," the 53 houses, the saltbox vernacular, the Newfoundland dialect, the
reclamation-of-ancestral-land narrative.

---

## Reading the two labels

Each direction is tagged:

- **[CARRY]** — takes a portable concept into a genuinely new subject. Fails if it needs
  Newfoundland to work.
- **[LEAP]** — a new question the thesis never asked. Fails if it's only a re-skin of the old
  work.

Roughly half and half by design, so there's a real choice between *deepening the method* and
*breaking from it*.

---

## Ten directions

### 1. The Inaccessible Other — telepresence to a place *no one* is from  [CARRY]
Point the camera-as-surrogate at a site with no ancestral claim for anyone: the deep sea
floor, a glacier interior, a decommissioned reactor, orbit. Same apparatus (a body-less
device holding a foothold), stripped of the family narrative — testing whether presence-at-a-
distance still moves us when there is no "home" underneath. *Outputs: live/near-live
installation, kinetic data-object, web feed. Register: sublime, cool.*

### 2. Rooms of Strangers' Windows  [CARRY]
Cloudberry was one window. Invite *many* people worldwide to run a fixed-frame window
capture of their own view for a year, under one rigid protocol (à la Nixon's *Brown
Sisters*). The work becomes a chorus of quotidian sublimes — the method proven portable
because it holds across a thousand unrelated frames. *Outputs: web commons + IIIF archive +
book + gallery wall of synchronized "noons." Register: tender, communal.*

### 3. The Extracted Ritual, Anywhere  [CARRY]
Take the *Mug Up* operation — lift an intangible custom out of its architecture to reveal it —
and apply it to rituals with no Newfoundland tie: a shift-change, a prayer, a border crossing,
a video-call goodbye. Perform each one removed from its container. *Outputs: performance +
two-channel video, co-authored with the ritual's real practitioners. Register: tender.*

### 4. Presence Without Residency — the wandering agent  [LEAP]
A new question: *can a machine belong somewhere it only passes through?* Build a slow
autonomous agent (drift buoy, rover, shy robot) that moves through public or wild space,
holding a fleeting, non-owning presence — the opposite of Cloudberry's staked foothold.
Precedents: Norma Jeane's *ShyBot*, Gaetano Adi's *Guanaquerx*. *Outputs: field deployment +
gallery documentation + the returned, weathered object. Register: wonder; high build-risk —
prototype the smallest emotionally-complete version first.*

### 5. The Abundance-Sublime of Data You Can't See  [LEAP]
Turn the fixed-frame-over-time method away from landscape entirely: apply it to a stream of
invisible signal — a body's biometrics over a year, a city's night sounds, a single radio
frequency. The awe-by-accumulation engine, no horizon at all. *Outputs: generative print
series, immersive sound room, book. Register: sublime, abstract.*

### 6. Home as Membrane, Not Structure  [CARRY]
Push "home is not defined by physical structure" into new form: Do Ho Suh-style translucent
membranes of *non-domestic* thresholds — a hospital room, a first apartment, a car you lived
out of. The portable-home concept freed from the saltbox. *Outputs: immersive sewn/scrim
installation, walk-through. Register: tender.*

### 7. The Surrogate That Outlives Its Subject  [LEAP]
A new ethical question the closed archive opened: *what does a recording device owe the person
or place it outlasts?* Commission or build surrogate devices that are explicitly designed to
keep witnessing after their subject is gone — then exhibit the devices themselves as mourners.
Precedent register: Boltanski's shrines, Stine Deja's *Grave Matters*. *Outputs: vitrine /
installation of "witness objects" + oral archive. Register: tender, elegiac.*

### 8. Translation Failure as the Work  [LEAP]
Bowen prizes the *imperfect* translation where the machine can't fully mimic nature. Make that
gap the subject: a whole body of work about **what is lost when a place, language, or gesture
is converted to data** — deliberately foregrounding the seams, dropouts, and mistranslations.
A conceptual leap from "represent a place" to "portray the act of representation failing."
*Outputs: kinetic pieces that visibly fail, glitched composites, essayistic install.
Register: cool, wonder-at-the-edges.*

### 9. Two Places at Once — the diaspora bridge  [CARRY]
The "third space of hybridity" without Newfoundland: link two real, live, distant sites
(where a diaspora community's "here" and "there" both exist) so each animates the other in a
shared space — a room in one city breathing with the weather/sound of another. Consider
ceding network control to the communities themselves (the IsumaTV lesson). *Outputs:
two-site telematic installation, co-authored. Register: tender, political-but-not-preachy.*

### 10. The Studio as Instrument — a durational self-portrait  [LEAP]
Turn the method inward, the one place he can't leave: the studio / the body / the daily
practice itself, recorded under a fixed frame for years (Sudek's window, On Kawara's daily
rule). A leap from documenting an ancestral place to documenting the *maker* as the place that
persists. Quietly answers the unresolved "Come From Away" identity question by making the
self the site. *Outputs: lifelong fixed-frame archive, book, single-channel piece.
Register: tender, wry.*

---

## How to choose

- If the pull is to **deepen the method** → lead with a **[CARRY]**: #2 (Strangers' Windows),
  #3 (Extracted Ritual), or #9 (Diaspora Bridge).
- If the pull is a **real break** → lead with a **[LEAP]**: #4 (Wandering Agent), #7
  (Surrogate That Outlives), or #8 (Translation Failure).
- If you want a **quiet, low-risk first step that is still new** → #10 (Studio as Instrument)
  or #5 (Data Abundance-Sublime).

A natural trilogy: one **[CARRY]** to prove the method travels, one **[LEAP]** to open a new
question, and one that reconnects the two — the same shape the Newfoundland work took as it
moved from observing (*Sublime*) to inhabiting (*Mug Up*).
