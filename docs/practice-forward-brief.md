# Practice Forward Brief — Cloudberry / Pinchard's Island lane

> **Not an engineering backlog.** Cloudberry remains a closed archive
> product; see [PRODUCT.md](PRODUCT.md). This brief is artistic research for possible
> future chapters or sibling work.

A research brief for continuing and extending the Newfoundland body of work now that
Cloudberry is a closed archive. Grounded in a close reading of the MFA thesis
*Re-presenting Place: Reviving Newfoundland's Disappearing Culture* (2021), the field
guide *How to Get to Pinchard's Island*, and the ten statements at adamsim.ms.

- **Tone north star:** wonder, sublimity, tenderness.
- **Off-limits:** NFT drops, climate-campaign framing, tourism bait.
- **Status:** Cloudberry is closed (solar/camera gone, no planned return). New work may
  remix the archive; sibling projects (Adrift, Dory, Waves) stay separate but can collide
  with it.

---

## 1. The conceptual spine

The whole practice turns on one sentence from the thesis:

> "How do I represent a place that no longer exists?"

That artistic question is a stand-in for an identity question stated twice and never
resolved — *"am I a Newfoundlander, or have I become a Come From Away?"* ("within myself,
I may never be at peace with the answer"). Underneath both runs the most portable engine
in the work, taken from the about page: **investigating which aspects of culture are
portable and which are rooted in place.**

Now that the camera is dead, a fourth question sharpens: **can technology let you *be*
somewhere you are absent from — and is that a gift or a consolation prize?** The work has
always leaned on Virilio's telepresence and Ang's "third space of hybridity" and never
fully decided.

### The closure changes the meaning of the archive

Cloudberry's original claim was an "uninterrupted," "continuous" foothold — a *political
act* of occupation. Closure converts that ongoing occupation into a **finite corpus** that
now behaves exactly like the historical resettlement photographs the work has always
leaned on. The living feed has become a relic; the work's own critique of photography
("references the past's irretrievability") now applies to Cloudberry itself.

Two consequences worth honoring rather than hiding:

- **The camera's death mirrors the resettlement.** The surrogate body that "stands in for
  the photographer" has itself been resettled and silenced. There is a genuine, un-forced
  elegy in the last frame, the dead solar panel, the final upload.
- **Doris named the ending.** "D'once y'done wit'da island, dat'll be it… Y'r de last of
  dem t'be'ut dere." The field guide's last line — *"It's time for me to resettle."* — was
  written before it turned prophetic. Treat closure as an ending she named, not an outage
  to patch.

## 2. Place-bound vs portable (so we know what the next chapter is made of)

**Place-bound (needs NL / Pinchard's / resettlement specifically):** the Resettlement
Acts as literal event (the $100, the 53 floated saltbox homes, names "expunged from
maps," the graveyard); Doris as named collaborator and ethical center; the inhaled
affirmative "yes" and other dialect vestiges; the 53 black spruce trees (one per house,
GPS-mapped); the phrase "no great future"; the cabin built in defiance of the Acts;
Ktaqmkuk / Beothuk / Mi'kmaq land and Adam's own unreconciled Mi'kmaq descent.

**Portable (travel without the island — carried into the companion brief):** data-driven
telepresence (a distant place moving an object here); the abundance-sublime (fixed frame +
accumulated time); the *rückenfigur* seen from behind; extracting a ritual from its
architecture; home as ephemeral rather than structural; the camera-as-surrogate for an
absent body.

**Open threads to hold, not solve:** the Mi'kmaq / Ktaqmkuk debt the thesis already
commits to; settler reclamation sitting uneasily beside Indigenous erasure; the family's
authority over their own story (the cabin was voted sold "after the fact"); the
insider/outsider "Come From Away" wound as productive, not fixable.

---

## 3. Productive tensions to preserve (not resolve)

- **Presence vs absence** — elaborate tech to be present where you are absent, yet the
  field guide concludes the only way to truly feel the island is to leave it for good.
- **Preservation vs letting go** — the mission is to make the culture "persist," yet the
  arc ends in selling, resettling, and the admission that "the memory of the place is
  stronger than the place itself."
- **Photography as lie vs truth** — adopts Virilio's "photography lies," never abandons
  the medium.
- **Wonder vs grief** — Sublime's fog is "almost playful"; Cloudberry is awe; Resettlement
  is mournful. Tenderness sits on top of catastrophe. Keep both.

## 4. Artist & exhibition references

Verified references organized by the formal problem each one solves. Use these to steal
*structure*, not subject.

### Fixed frame / same view over time
- **Nicholas Nixon, *The Brown Sisters* (1975–ongoing)** — one rule, one subject, one image
  a year for decades. Steal: a rigid, publicly-stated ritual constraint that lets a
  place/family reveal itself only across years. [moma.org](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/45306)
- **Michael Wesely, *Open Shutter* (2001–2004)** — exposures up to 34 months; only what
  persists registers. Steal: letting one frame *integrate* months rather than sample
  instants — a formal model for a Cloudberry composite. [moma.org](https://www.moma.org/calendar/exhibitions/119)
- **Josef Sudek, *The Window of My Studio* (1940–1954)** — 14 years through one Prague
  pane, "both frames the outside world and serves as a barrier." The definitive case for
  the domestic window as a lifelong looking-machine and quiet political shelter. [noma.org](https://noma.org/object-lesson-the-window-of-my-studio-by-josef-sudek/)

### Remote sensing / telepresence
- **Ken Goldberg & Joseph Santarromana, *The Telegarden* (1995–2004)** — web users tend a
  real garden via robot arm; telepresence as *care* and stewardship. The ancestor of
  camera-as-foothold. [goldberg.berkeley.edu](https://goldberg.berkeley.edu/garden/)
- **Paul Sermon, *Telematic Dreaming* (1992)** — two beds, one projected body; "the image
  prevails over the thing" made tender and literal. [paulsermon.org](https://www.paulsermon.org/dream/dream.html)
- **Wafaa Bilal, *3rdi* (2010–2011)** — head-mounted camera, one image/minute to a live
  site, to archive "places we leave behind… sometimes by choice… sometimes forced." Almost
  a schematic of Cloudberry's own apparatus, fused with displacement. [wafaabilal.com](http://wafaabilal.com/thirdi/)
- **Zacharias Kunuk / Igloolik Isuma, *IsumaTV* (2008–ongoing)** — an Inuit-owned network
  giving a remote community presence "in their own language." Telepresence as political
  foothold; the infrastructure *is* the artwork. Consider who controls the network. [isuma.tv](https://www.isuma.tv/about-us)

### Displacement / resettlement / erased places
- **Scott Walden, *Unsettled / Places Lost* (1997–2003)** — the literal precedent: 48
  photographs of Newfoundland communities emptied by post-Confederation resettlement, paired
  with oral testimony. Study how ruin-image meets recovered spoken memory. [scottwalden.net](https://scottwalden.net/unsettled.html)
- **Krzysztof Wodiczko, public projections (1980–ongoing)** — monuments as "megaphones for
  the powerless," projecting displaced testimony onto surviving form. A template for
  memorial-as-testimony in a register of dignity. [art21.org](https://art21.org/artist/krzysztof-wodiczko/)
- **Stan Douglas, *Nu•tka•* (1996)** — two video tracks of a contested BC coast sync only at
  rare moments; "Canadian Gothic." Interlacing/desync as a metaphor for a place seen through
  irreconcilable memories. [depont.nl](https://www.depont.nl/en/exhibitions/stan-douglas)
- **Do Ho Suh, *Home Within Home* (2013)** — walk-in translucent sewn-fabric houses; a
  departed home made portable, spectral, enterable. A masterclass for Cabin / ghost houses. [americanart.si.edu](https://americanart.si.edu/exhibitions/suh)

### Data → motion / kinetic sculpture linked to elsewhere
- **David Bowen, *tele-present water* (2011) / *tele-present wind* (2018/21)** — the closest
  formal cousin to Driftwood and Adrift: a servo grid rising to live NOAA buoy data (even
  Mars-rover wind), framed as "the feeling of being present in a faraway place," prizing the
  *imperfect* translation. Study the mechanism and the scaling. [dwbowen.com](https://www.dwbowen.com/telepresent-wind)
- **Natalie Jeremijenko, *Live Wire / Dangling String* (1995)** — one motorized cord tied to
  network traffic; "calm technology," an unseen remote signal made into peripheral motion.
  Restraint as strategy. [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natalie_Jeremijenko)

### Abundance / archive as sublime
- **Penelope Umbrico, *Suns from Sunsets from Flickr* (2006–ongoing)** — thousands of cropped
  suns, title updates to the live tag-count. The quotidian sublime as networked abundance. [penelopeumbrico.net](http://www.penelopeumbrico.net/index.php/project/suns-from-sunsets-from-flickr/)
- **Bernd & Hilla Becher, Typologies / *Water Towers* (1959–2000s)** — identical framing of
  vanishing forms, gridded so sameness reveals difference; archive as preservation. The
  grammar for monumentalizing humble endangered vernacular (stages, outbuildings). [moma.org](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/49624)
- **Zoe Leonard, *You see I am here after all* (2008)** — ~4,000 Niagara postcards; a place
  rendered almost entirely through its own circulated images. Tender, longing, elegiac
  rather than promotional. [diaart.org](https://diaart.org/exhibition/exhibitions-projects/zoe-leonard-you-see-i-am-here-after-all-2008-exhibition)
- **On Kawara, *Today* series (1966–2013)** — one date painting per surviving day, boxed with
  that day's newspaper; the archive of ordinary days *is* the work. A rigorous engine for
  daily witnessing. [davidzwirner.com](https://www.davidzwirner.com/exhibitions/2024/on-kawara-london)

### Cabin / window / domestic frame as instrument
- **Roni Horn, *Vatnasafn / Library of Water* (2007)** — 24 glass columns of glacial
  meltwater in a former library + a companion oral archive of locals' weather memories. A
  whole-ecosystem model: a fixed room that turns light/weather into experience, plus voices. [artangel.org.uk](https://www.artangel.org.uk/project/library-of-water/)
- **James Turrell, Skyspace (1974–)** — a room whose only picture is a knife-edged aperture
  onto sky; the frame is empty, changing light is the content. The purest quotidian-sublime
  machine. [massmoca.org](https://massmoca.org/mass-moca-to-open-a-skyspace-by-james-turrell/)

### Memorial / afterlife for missing forms
- **Rachel Whiteread, *House* (1993)** — a concrete cast of a condemned house's *interior
  air*; a mute memorial to home, itself later demolished. Cast absence, not structure. [artangel.org.uk](https://www.artangel.org.uk/project/house/)
- **Gordon Matta-Clark, *Splitting* (1974)** — a demolition-bound house bisected so light
  knifes through; the film and fragments are its afterlife. Intervention on a doomed dwelling
  as memorial. [whitney.org](https://whitney.org/collection/works/12740)
- **Christian Boltanski, *Monuments / Personnes* (1985–2010)** — altar-archives of blurred
  re-photographed faces and second-hand clothing; humble materials + repetition + low light =
  a secular shrine to small memory. The toolkit for the afterlives of vanished people. [mariangoodman.com](https://www.mariangoodman.com/artists/christian-boltanski/)

### Impressionist serial light (art-historical bridge)
- **Claude Monet, *Haystacks* / *Rouen Cathedral* (1890–94)** — one motif across light and
  season, many canvases at once: "what I want to reproduce is what exists between the motif
  and me." The origin point of fixed-frame abundance. [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haystacks_\(Monet_series\))
- **Hiroshi Sugimoto, *Seascapes* (1980–ongoing)** — one compositional law (sea, sky, centered
  horizon) worldwide; includes a plate titled *Atlantic Ocean, New Foundland, 1982* — a
  literal Simms coordinate. Discipline of a single fixed frame as a meditation on memory. [metmuseum.org](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/267267)
- **David Hockney, *The Arrival of Spring* / *Woldgate Woods* (2006–2011)** — one Yorkshire
  road across seasons, ending in a slightly-desynced grid of screens. The bridge from
  Impressionist seriality to camera/screen arrays. [hockney2022.ch](https://www.hockney2022.ch/en/hockney/landschaft/)

*Screened out: climate-dashboard and tourism-boosting projects. Straight-swap alternatives
if you want to avoid even borderline register — Tacita Dean (*Disappearance at Sea*) for
Leonard; Reuben Margolin (wave kinetics) for Jeremijenko.*

## 5. Software capability map

Treat the archive as a *held thing* — one window, thousands of hours — and ask software to
deepen attention rather than explain it. Live data appears only as weather on the glass,
never as a chart.

- **Archive-as-instrument** (scrubbers, atlases, flip-year, similarity, hour-across-years) —
  turns a fixed corpus into something *playable*; the viewer becomes a time-traveler at one
  window. Precedents: CLIP-embedding map browsers (PhotoMapAI, ImageSpace), Urocissa for
  million-frame scrub, Manovich/Stefaner's *Multiplicity*, Marclay's *The Clock* as the
  real-time-mapping model. *The heart of the closed archive.*
- **Averaging / composite portraits of one view** — the sublime of the aggregate; one image
  that is *all* mornings at once. Precedents: Jason Salavon, Corinne Vionnet, Penelope
  Umbrico. Natural still + book output ("the median cabin," "the average of every snowfall").
- **Live data → behavior (no dashboard)** — puppet-strings; the ocean animates the object
  with no numbers shown. Precedents: David Bowen's buoy→servo grids, Shannon Murphy's
  *Data Wave*. The exact grammar for Adrift/Driftwood. When the buoy goes silent, the *silence*
  is the poem.
- **Generative afterlife of a closed corpus (labeled synthetic)** — the archive dreaming past
  its own end; new mornings that never happened. Borrow the latent-walk aesthetic of Refik
  Anadol's *Unsupervised* — but *not* its NFT distribution. Keep it a separate, seam-visible,
  wall-labeled companion.
- **Immersive window / room software** — the cabin reconstituted around a body; a room that
  is only a window and its weather. Turrell as archetype; TouchDesigner / edge-blended
  projection as the build.
- **Exhibition playback (single/dual-channel, loanable "month of days")** — reliability *is*
  tenderness toward the work; it must travel and survive without you in the room. Hardened
  Pi / BrightSign appliances with PTP sync.
- **Remix / open-call platform** — the archive as commons; wonder multiplies when strangers
  scrub your mornings. IIIF (Rijksstudio model) gives deep-zoom, stable URLs, ready viewers.
- **Book / print pipeline** — the archive made touchable and slow; the object outlives the
  server. basil.js (Processing-in-InDesign), Schmitt's *Computed Curation* sequencing,
  Kessels' *24 HRS in Photos* as the overload extreme.
- **Composite worlds** — Adrift's house or a boat drifting *inside* Cloudberry's window.
  3D Gaussian splatting / photogrammetry to reconstruct the view, then drop matched-light
  objects in. The bridge linking Cloudberry ↔ Adrift ↔ Cabin.

**Stack affordances (not a shopping list):** WebGL/WebGPU (PixiJS for 50k-thumbnail atlases,
Three.js for room/composite scenes, IIIF/OpenSeadragon for deep-zoom); **CLIP embeddings +
brute-force nearest-neighbor** as the *honest* backbone of every "similar morning" gesture
(keeps real frames on screen, no invention, no vector DB needed for thousands of images);
UMAP/t-SNE + HDBSCAN for the constellation view; StyleGAN fine-tune reserved *only* for the
labeled "Dreaming" companion (prefer interpolation *between real frames* when staying near
truth); TouchDesigner for the always-on room; ESP32-S3 / Teensy + PCA9685 servo drivers as
Driftwood/Adrift's nervous system.

## 6. Hardware capability map

The camera's failure is the *asset*, not a fault to engineer away. Nothing here assumes a
return trip or on-site maintenance.

- **Fixed remote cameras / solar / cellular successors** — presence-at-a-distance without a
  body. Precedents: Rob Ray's Mojave *Solarcam*; Regina Valkenborgh's 8-year pinhole
  solargraph. *Cloudberry-adjacent.* Lowest-risk, highest-tenderness: a **solargraph vigil**
  that needs no network at all.
- **Multi-sensor cabins** — audio, tide, wind, temperature as *sculpture drivers*, not just
  photo. Extends Cabin/Driftwood: the window that listens as well as looks.
- **Autonomous / semi-autonomous agents** (drones, rovers, floating robots) as "presence
  without residency." Precedents: Norma Jeane's *ShyBot*, Gaetano Adi's *Guanaquerx*,
  Gapševičius's *Pond Says Hello*. *New; highest build-risk* — prototype the smallest
  emotionally-complete version first.
- **Gallery kinetics driven by distant or archival data** — Bowen / von Bismarck's Atlantic
  sister-buoy lineage. *Extends Driftwood/Adrift.*
- **Projection / window / room machines** — the live-plus-remembered window; Cabin's core.
- **Print/object fabrication from archives** — plotters, books, card decks, dyed edges; Simon
  Kirby's plotter field-recordings as reference. The archive made into a held object.
- **Dead hardware as relic / funeral / vitrine** — the actual failed camera, solar panel,
  last SD card in a vitrine or wake. Precedent register: Stine Deja's *Grave Matters*.
  *Lowest-risk, reads immediately.*
- **Collaborative / family ritual devices** — objects that invite Doris-style co-authorship
  and oral capture.
- **Hybrid: physical object + networked ghost** — a gallery object shadowed by a live or
  archival data-double.
- **Speculative** — robots that tend memorials; a camera that "dreams" after death; ghost-house
  outlines (cf. Robert Hengeveld's resettlement outlines).

## 7. Anti-patterns (don't go here)

- **Dashboard creep** — any visible gauge, wind readout, or "source: NOAA 44097" chrome kills
  the wonder. Data drives motion/color/tempo only.
- **Message-ification** — no "sea levels rising" overlays, counters, or calls to action.
  Climate is felt, never argued.
- **NFT / scarcity mechanics** — borrow the *technique* (latent walks) never the minting.
- **Infinite doomscroll UX** — endless scroll flattens the archive into content. Prefer
  bounded gestures (a day, a month, an hour-across-years) with friction and pause.
- **Over-smoothing the generative layer** until synthetic frames pass as real — erodes the
  archive's documentary truth. Always label; keep a visible seam.
- **Fragile always-on installs** — assume the internet drops and buoys go silent; design the
  graceful, poetic fallback (the house drifts on the *last* reading, slowly stilling).
- **Premature ML** — reaching for a fine-tune when nearest-neighbor retrieval would do; it's
  more honest, cheaper, and keeps real frames on screen.
- **Resurrecting the camera** — restoring the "live foothold" undoes the elegy Doris named.

## 8. Ten directions to explore next

Brush strokes only — concept, form, output, rough effort. All stay in the NL / Cloudberry
lane and honor wonder / sublimity / tenderness.

1. **The Year Scrubber** — one web page, one window, a single horizontal gesture flying
   through 12 months of hourly frames; flick fast and weather becomes wind, hold still and an
   afternoon breathes. Press-and-hold "hold this light" snaps to every similar hour (CLIP
   retrieval, no synthesis). *Output: web piece + gallery projection. Effort: low–medium.*

2. **The Median Cabin** — quietly generative stills: mean/median composites binned by weather
   ("all snowfalls," "every clear noon," "all fog"). The aggregate sublime — no single moment
   survives yet all are present. *Output: print series + book chapter. Effort: low–medium.*

3. **Hour-Across-Years Loom** — pick a clock-time; weave that same minute from every year into
   a slow vertical column — a fugue of one instant. *Output: tall prints + screen. Effort: low.*

4. **Constellation Atlas** — the whole archive as a UMAP starfield you wander, clusters left
   unlabeled so meaning stays felt not named; click a star, that morning opens full-bleed.
   *Output: web + museum touch-wall. Effort: medium.*

5. **Last Transmission** — a single-channel gallery loop of the final frames, then permanent
   black / no-carrier; the dead solar panel and last SD card in a companion vitrine. The
   camera's death honored as content. *Output: single-channel piece + relic. Effort: low.*

6. **Cloudberry Dreaming (labeled synthetic)** — a latent walk trained on the archive,
   projected with a visible seam and a wall label: *these mornings never happened.* Idle-state
   dreaming, no chain/NFT layer. *Output: projection companion. Effort: high.*

7. **The Boat in the Window** — Gaussian-splat reconstruct the cabin view, then let Adrift's
   house or a Dory drift *through* archived frames, light-matched and uncanny. The sibling
   projects meet inside the closed archive. *Output: web scene / installation. Effort: high.*

8. **The Automatic Book (one window, one year)** — a basil.js / *Computed Curation* pipeline
   sequencing a print book by light and weather; one edition per year — the server's mortal,
   holdable counterpart. Consider bakeapple-dyed edges. *Output: book / doorstop object.
   Effort: medium.*

9. **The 53 Trees, Walkable** — turn the existing tree-geodata (one black spruce per resettled
   house) into a walkable map / sound-walk / projection: a memorial you move through rather
   than read. Advance the "no great future" defiance into navigable form. *Output: web map +
   gallery floor projection + optional on-site. Effort: medium.*

10. **Mug Up, Re-sounded** — pair the surviving oral fragments (the inhaled "yes," Doris's
    verdict) with matching weather days from the archive; a two-channel piece where voice meets
    the light of a named day. Honors Doris's authority and the intangible-culture thread.
    *Output: two-channel video/sound. Effort: medium.*
